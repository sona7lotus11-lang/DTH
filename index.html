What is Archaeology

The scientific study of material remains such as tools pottery jewelry stone walls and monuments of past human life and activities

Why is it even important?

The reason why archaeology is so important is that it teaches humans about their past in the areas where they live. Archaeologists learn to understand why, how and to a rough estimate when such influential events happened in our past and how some of those events continue to impact our lives today.

Looking into how agricultural tools developed alongside the rise of permanent villages and cities helps us understand why civilizations came into being and why some eventually fell apart. When we dig into how and why cultures evolved in the past it gives us a clearer picture of human behavior and the way we influence our environment.

A lot of our future decisions are shaped by the insights archaeologists have gained from those who lived before us. By better understanding our history we deepen our knowledge of cultural shifts happening now and what might happen down the road which helps us prepare for and address the social, political , environmental and demographic challenges we face today.

Archaeology isn't just one field to be discovered its eclectic for our understanding of history

Many people picture archaeology as just one field where you are in desert areas digging up and finding artifacts. It is our main idea of archaeology as it’s very important yet if we really look at it it’s so much more than that. Archaeology has so many parts to it which usually goes unnoticed. When researchers study what they have found it’s not all just ancient archaeologists and or architectural buildings. Archaeologists can concentrate on a specific area within their field. This involves focusing on certain methods of analysis, types of artifacts, specific cultures or particular historical time frames. One of the types of researchers study is based on geography, geology, geophysics and other Earth sciences. They are called geoarcheologists studying and examining the land features of what they used to be like.

New Discoveries

Last undiscovered tomb of Tutankhamun dynasty found

For more than a century the sands of Egypt have guarded a royal secret that many believed was lost to time. Now a monumental find has finally broken that silence unearthing a hidden legacy that hasnt been seen since the legendary discovery of 1922.

A Human Face Was Carved Into This Stone Pillar in Turkey 11000 Years Ago

Deep within the rugged Stone Hills of Turkey a silent sentinel has emerged from an 11000 year old slumber to finally stare back at the modern world. This unprecedented discovery at a legendary archaeological site offers a hauntingly personal connection to our earliest ancestors revealing a secret that has been hidden beneath the earth since the dawn of civilization.

Hymn to Babylon discovered revealing ancient Mesopotamian city life

Buried within a library said to have been hidden before the Great Flood a long lost voice has finally broken its two thousand year silence. This recovered masterpiece resurrected through a cutting edge technological breakthrough offers a rare and hauntingly beautiful window into a world once thought lost to history.

Oldest known rock art dated to 67800 years discovered in Sulawesi cave

For tens of thousands of years a ghostly imprint has waited in the shadows of a remote cave to tell a story of a world long forgotten. This haunting signature has finally emerged from the darkness shattering our understanding of when the human spirit first reached out to leave its mark on the world. Click above to learn more.

15 million year old bone tools discovered in Tanzania to rewrite the history of human evolution

Deep within a legendary African gorge a hidden collection of artifacts has finally come to light rewriting a million years of forgotten history in a single stroke. These tools carved with unexpected precision from the bones of long dead behemoths hint at a sophisticated mind that shouldnt have existed for another millennia.

This Ancient Pyramid Found in the Judean Desert May Have Been a Ptolemaic Tax Collectors Station

Perched on a desolate desert mound a strange stone sentinel has guarded a cache of forgotten treasures for over two thousand years. This mysterious structure once teeming with ancient life and bureaucracy now stands as a silent monument to a lost power that once controlled the very roads of the Judean wilderness.

Archaeologists Unearth the Ruins of a 2000 Year Old Roman Basilica Beneath an Office Building in London

Beneath the polished floors of a modern London financial hub a silent witness to a forgotten empire has finally been pulled from the shadows. This sprawling 2000 year old sanctuary of power once stood at the heart of a civilization waiting centuries for the light of day to find it once again
